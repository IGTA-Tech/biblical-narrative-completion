Copy this structure into `jewish_biblical_narrative_enhanced.md` and fill in each component.

### [BOOK NAME] Chapters [X-Y]: Contextual Analysis

**Narrative Summary:** [150-250 words - What happens in these chapters]

[Write a clear, engaging summary of the narrative. Include:
- Key events in chronological order
- Main characters and their actions
- Important dialogue or declarations
- Plot developments and turning points
- Outcome or consequences
- Transition to next section]

**Historical and Archaeological Context:** [250-400 words - Archaeological evidence and dating]

[CRITICAL: Include SPECIFIC citations. Name actual artifacts, sites, and scholars. Examples:
- "The Merneptah Stele (c. 1208 BCE) provides..."
- "Excavations at Tell es-Sultan (ancient Jericho) by Kathleen Kenyon (1952-1958)..."
- "The Dead Sea Scrolls (11QPsa) preserve..."
- "Mesopotamian texts from Mari (18th century BCE) document..."
- "The Babylonian Chronicles record..."

Include:
- Specific archaeological sites (tells, excavations)
- Named artifacts (steles, inscriptions, tablets, ostraca, papyri)
- Ancient texts (epics, annals, legal codes, letters)
- Scholarly debates (traditional vs. critical dating)
- Multiple perspectives on controversial topics
- Connections to ancient Near Eastern parallels]

**Cultural Practices Reflected:** [200-350 words - Ancient customs and daily life]

[Describe cultural elements visible in the text:
- Social structures (family, tribe, class)
- Religious practices (worship, festivals, sacrifices)
- Daily life (agriculture, commerce, crafts)
- Marriage and family customs
- Legal and judicial practices
- Economic systems
- Rites of passage
- Food and dining practices
- Clothing and adornment
- Compare with broader ancient Near Eastern culture]

**Political Situation:** [200-350 words - Geopolitical context]

[Analyze the political landscape:
- Governing structures (monarchy, judges, tribal)
- International relations (alliances, conflicts)
- Power dynamics and succession
- Vassal/suzerain relationships
- Treaty obligations
- Military context
- Administrative systems
- Territorial claims or disputes
- Leadership transitions
- Connection to broader regional powers (Egypt, Assyria, Babylon, Persia, Rome)]

**Theological Themes:** [250-400 words - Spiritual concepts and divine action]

[Explore theological dimensions:
- Divine character and attributes
- Covenant relationship
- Redemptive history development
- Sin and judgment
- Grace and mercy
- Worship and holiness
- Kingdom theology
- Messianic anticipation
- Faith and obedience
- Divine sovereignty vs. human responsibility
- Spiritual warfare
- Eschatological significance
- Use specific biblical references to support themes]

**Connection to Broader Narrative:** [250-400 words - How this fits the whole Bible story]

[Connect this section to the larger biblical narrative:
- Link to previous biblical events
- Anticipate future developments
- Show recurring patterns or motifs
- Connect Old Testament to New Testament
- Identify typological significance
- Trace themes through Scripture
- Show canonical placement importance
- Explain redemptive-historical significance
- Note intertextual connections

- Demonstrate narrative arc progression]

## Quality Checklist

Before committing, verify:

- [ ] **Narrative Summary**: 150-250 words, clear and engaging
- [ ] **Historical Context**: 250-400 words with 5+ specific citations
- [ ] **Cultural Practices**: 200-350 words with detailed customs
- [ ] **Political Situation**: 200-350 words with geopolitical analysis
- [ ] **Theological Themes**: 250-400 words with biblical support
- [ ] **Broader Connection**: 250-400 words showing canonical links
- [ ] **Archaeological Evidence**: At least 5 specific artifacts/sites named
- [ ] **Ancient Sources**: At least 2-3 ancient texts cited
- [ ] **Scholarly Perspectives**: At least 2-3 different viewpoints
- [ ] **Total Word Count**: 1,400+ words for the entire section
- [ ] **No Placeholders**: All `[...]` brackets removed
- [ ] **Clean Formatting**: Markdown properly formatted
- [ ] **Spell Check**: No typos or grammatical errors

## Quick Tips

**For Archaeological Context:**
- Search "archaeology [book name] [chapter]" for recent findings
- Check Bible Archaeology Review, Associates for Biblical Research
- Use Blue Letter Bible for Strong's references
- Cross-reference with IVP Bible Background Commentary

**For Cultural Practices:**
- Compare with other ancient Near Eastern cultures
- Note what's unique to Israel vs. what's common
- Explain WHY practices matter theologically

**For Theological Themes:**
- Ask: What does this reveal about God?
- Ask: How does this advance the redemptive story?
- Connect themes across Testament boundaries

**For Narrative Connection:**
- Trace key themes (covenant, kingdom, exodus, exile, return)
- Look for foreshadowing and fulfillment
- Identify character types that recur
